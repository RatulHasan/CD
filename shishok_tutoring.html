Shishok Tutoring:

Knowledge of reading. writing and arithmetic is a fundamental human right of a citizen in any modern state. Fortunately, the constitution of People's Republic of Bangladesh pledges 'a uniform, mass oriented and universal system of education' to all its citizens. Unfortunately, nearly half our population is still unlettered even in so many years since our independence in 1971. This is a big obstacle because Bangladesh has no alternative to an educated human resource achieved through universal education for its development. We know, Japan achieved hundred percent literacy a hundred years ago. This achievement was the result of a social movement, "no community, no family of the community and no member of the family will remain illiterate". This was made possible in just forty years with the support of the state. With few natural resources, this educated human resource became the key to its present amazing development. In Bangladesh Compulsory Primary Education Act was enacted in 1990. An Education Policy was prepared and passed in 2010. People's awareness about education has increased to a very good extent.
